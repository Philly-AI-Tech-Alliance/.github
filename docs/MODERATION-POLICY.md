# Moderation Policy

**Philly-AI-Tech-Alliance**

## Overview

This document outlines our community moderation policy, including how we maintain safe and productive spaces, respond to issues, and handle violations of our Code of Conduct.

Our moderation philosophy is proactive, fair, and focused on education over punishment.

## Moderation Team

### Structure

**Core Moderators** (3-5 people)
- Selected from Core Team and senior Maintainers
- Minimum 6 months of active community participation
- Demonstrated sound judgment and conflict resolution skills
- Diverse representation across demographics
- Appointed by Core Team consensus

**Platform Moderators** (5-10 people)
- Focus on specific platforms (GitHub, Slack, LinkedIn, Events)
- Support Core Moderators
- Handle routine issues
- Escalate complex situations
- Nominated by existing moderators

### Responsibilities

**All Moderators**:
- Monitor community spaces
- Respond to reports promptly
- Apply policy consistently
- Document incidents
- Maintain confidentiality
- Participate in moderator meetings
- Continuous training on best practices

**Core Moderators** (additionally):
- Handle escalated issues
- Make final decisions on bans
- Update moderation policy
- Train new moderators
- Represent moderation in governance

### Moderator Selection

**Criteria**:
- 6+ months active community member
- Demonstrated maturity and judgment
- Strong communication skills
- Understanding of Code of Conduct
- Ability to remain neutral
- Time commitment: 3-5 hours/week

**Process**:
1. Open application or nomination
2. Review by existing moderators
3. Interview with Core Moderators
4. Trial period (1 month)
5. Full appointment

### Moderator Accountability

Moderators must:
- Declare conflicts of interest
- Recuse themselves when necessary
- Follow moderation guidelines
- Avoid abuse of power
- Accept oversight from Core Team

**Removal**: Moderators can be removed for:
- Abuse of power
- Consistent poor judgment
- Violations of Code of Conduct
- Extended inactivity
- Personal request

## Guiding Principles

### 1. **Education First**
- Prioritize learning over punishment
- Explain why something is problematic
- Provide resources for improvement
- Give benefit of the doubt initially

### 2. **Proportional Response**
- Match response to severity of issue
- Consider context and history
- Start with warnings when appropriate
- Escalate only when necessary

### 3. **Consistency**
- Apply rules fairly across all members
- Document decisions for precedent
- Regular moderator calibration
- Clear guidelines, not arbitrary decisions

### 4. **Transparency**
- Public moderation guidelines
- General communication about actions
- Anonymous case studies for learning
- Regular policy reviews

### 5. **Privacy**
- Protect reporter identity
- Keep details confidential
- Share only what's necessary
- Secure storage of reports

### 6. **Restorative Justice**
- Focus on repairing harm
- Path back for violators (when appropriate)
- Community healing
- Learning opportunities

## Moderation Guidelines

### When to Moderate

**Always Moderate**:
- Harassment or bullying
- Hate speech or discrimination
- Spam or malicious content
- Doxxing or privacy violations
- Threats of violence
- Sexual content or advances
- Impersonation
- Illegal activity

**Often Moderate**:
- Persistent off-topic posts
- Excessive self-promotion
- Disruptive behavior
- Unconstructive criticism
- Ignoring maintainer guidance
- Repeated policy violations

**Consider Context**:
- Heated but substantive debates
- Cultural misunderstandings
- Language barriers
- First-time minor infractions
- Good-faith mistakes

**Generally Don't Moderate**:
- Technical disagreements
- Respectful debate
- Constructive criticism
- Different opinions
- Appropriate humor

## Response Process

### Step 1: Assessment

When an issue is reported or observed:

1. **Review** the situation thoroughly
   - Read full context
   - Check user history
   - Review similar past cases
   - Consider all perspectives

2. **Classify** the severity
   - **Minor**: First offense, easily correctable
   - **Moderate**: Repeated or more serious issue
   - **Severe**: Serious violation, immediate harm
   - **Critical**: Illegal activity, safety threats

3. **Determine** appropriate response
   - Consider the guidelines below
   - Consult with other moderators if uncertain
   - Document reasoning

### Step 2: Action

Based on severity, take appropriate action:

#### **Minor Issues** (Level 1)
**Examples**: Off-topic post, minor tone issue, first-time guideline miss

**Response**:
- Private message or gentle public comment
- Explain the issue
- Point to relevant guidelines
- No formal record (unless repeated)

**Template**:
```
Hi [name], thanks for your contribution. Just a friendly note that
[specific behavior] doesn't quite fit our community guidelines because
[reason]. Here's a link to our guidelines: [link]. Looking forward to
your continued participation!
```

---

#### **Moderate Issues** (Level 2)
**Examples**: Repeated minor issues, disruptive behavior, ignoring warnings

**Response**:
- Formal written warning
- Remove problematic content
- Temporary restrictions (3-7 days)
- Document in moderation log
- Notify Core Moderators

**Template**:
```
This is a formal warning regarding [specific behavior] in [location].
This violates our Code of Conduct section on [section]. Your [previous
warnings/context]. Going forward, we expect [expected behavior].
Further violations may result in temporary suspension. If you have
questions, please reach out to [moderator contact].
```

---

#### **Severe Issues** (Level 3)
**Examples**: Harassment, hate speech, sustained violations, major disruption

**Response**:
- Immediate content removal
- Temporary ban (7-30 days)
- Written explanation sent
- Documented in permanent log
- Core Moderator review required
- Community may be notified (without details)

**Template**:
```
Due to [specific violation] of our Code of Conduct, your access to
[platform/all platforms] has been temporarily suspended for [duration].
This decision was made because [reason]. During this time, [restrictions].
To have access reinstated after this period, we expect [requirements].
You may appeal this decision by [appeal process].
```

---

#### **Critical Issues** (Level 4)
**Examples**: Threats, illegal content, severe harassment, doxxing, impersonation

**Response**:
- Immediate permanent ban from all platforms
- All content removed
- Authorities contacted if necessary
- Organization leadership notified
- Public statement (if warranted)
- No automatic right to appeal (case-by-case)

**Template**:
```
Due to [specific severe violation], your participation in all Philly AI
Tech Alliance spaces has been permanently terminated. This decision is
final and was made because [brief reason]. [If applicable: We have
reported this to relevant authorities.] [If applicable: You may request
an appeal by following this process: [process].]
```

---

### Step 3: Documentation

All moderation actions must be documented:

**Required Information**:
- Date and time
- Platform/location
- Moderator(s) involved
- User(s) involved
- Description of issue
- Evidence (screenshots, links)
- Action taken
- Reasoning
- Follow-up required

**Storage**:
- Private moderation log (secure, access-controlled)
- Retention: 2 years
- Regular audits by Core Team

---

### Step 4: Follow-up

After taking action:

1. **Monitor** for compliance
2. **Support** user in improving (when appropriate)
3. **Check-in** with affected parties
4. **Review** after ban period
5. **Update** community if needed

## Response SLAs

Service Level Agreements for moderation response:

### Critical Issues (Level 4)
- **Response Time**: Within 1 hour during business hours, 4 hours off-hours
- **Resolution Time**: Immediate action, full investigation within 24 hours
- **Coverage**: 24/7 emergency contact available

### Severe Issues (Level 3)
- **Response Time**: Within 4 hours
- **Resolution Time**: Within 24 hours
- **Coverage**: Daily monitoring

### Moderate Issues (Level 2)
- **Response Time**: Within 24 hours
- **Resolution Time**: Within 48 hours
- **Coverage**: Business days (Mon-Fri)

### Minor Issues (Level 1)
- **Response Time**: Within 48 hours
- **Resolution Time**: Within 72 hours
- **Coverage**: Best effort

**Note**: These are targets, not guarantees. All moderators are volunteers. We'll do our best to respond quickly, but appreciate patience.

### Business Hours Definition
- **Timezone**: US Eastern Time (Philadelphia local)
- **Hours**: Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 6 PM ET
- **Exceptions**: Major holidays, community events (extended coverage)

## Escalation Paths

### When to Escalate

Escalate to Core Moderators when:
- Severity is uncertain
- High-profile community member involved
- Precedent-setting situation
- Potential for significant community impact
- Legal implications
- Moderator conflict of interest
- Appeals

### When to Escalate to Core Team

Escalate to full Core Team when:
- Potential legal liability
- Major public relations impact
- Core Team member involved
- Organization-wide policy implications
- Request for permanent ban
- Systemic issues identified

### Escalation Process

1. **Platform Moderator** observes/receives report
2. **Assess** and take immediate action if needed
3. **Document** thoroughly
4. **Notify** Core Moderator within SLA
5. **Collaborate** on resolution
6. **Escalate further** if needed
7. **Implement** decision
8. **Follow up** as required

## Appeals Process

We believe in second chances and fair process.

### Who Can Appeal

- Anyone who received a warning or ban
- Reporters unsatisfied with outcome (in limited cases)

### Grounds for Appeal

Valid reasons:
- New evidence not previously considered
- Procedural error in investigation
- Disproportionate punishment
- Mistaken identity
- Context not properly understood

Invalid reasons:
- Disagreement with policy
- "Everyone does it"
- Not knowing the rules
- Remorse alone (though it helps)

### Appeals Process

1. **Submit** appeal to appeals@philly-ai-tech.org
   - Within 14 days of action
   - Include:
     - Your account information
     - Action you're appealing
     - Grounds for appeal
     - New evidence/information
     - What outcome you seek

2. **Review** by uninvolved moderators
   - Different moderators than original case
   - Access to all documentation
   - May request additional information
   - 7 business days to review

3. **Decision**
   - Uphold original action
   - Reduce penalty
   - Overturn action
   - Additional remedies

4. **Notification**
   - Written decision with reasoning
   - Implemented immediately
   - Logged in records
   - Final (no further appeals)

### Appeals Timeline

- **Submission Window**: 14 days from action
- **Acknowledgment**: Within 2 business days
- **Decision**: Within 7 business days of acknowledgment
- **Total Process**: ~10 business days

**Note**: During appeal, original action remains in effect unless immediate harm is identified.

## Transparency & Accountability

### Public Transparency

We commit to:

**Quarterly Reports**:
- Number of incidents by severity
- Types of violations
- Actions taken (anonymized)
- Appeals outcomes
- Policy updates

**Case Studies** (anonymized):
- Educational examples
- How policies were applied
- Learning opportunities
- No identifying information

**Policy Updates**:
- Public changelog
- Rationale for changes
- Community input opportunities

### What We Don't Share Publicly

- Identity of involved parties
- Specific details of cases
- Private communications
- Reporter information
- Ongoing investigations

### Moderator Accountability

Moderators are accountable through:
- Peer review of decisions
- Quarterly Core Team audits
- Community feedback mechanisms
- Transparent guidelines
- Appeals process
- Training requirements

### Community Feedback

Help us improve:
- Annual moderation survey
- Feedback form always available
- Quarterly town hall discussion
- RFC for major policy changes

## Special Situations

### Cross-Platform Issues

When issues span multiple platforms:
- Coordinate action across platforms
- Ban applies to all community spaces
- Document in central log
- Consistent messaging

### External Behavior

Community members' behavior outside our spaces:
- Generally not moderated
- Exception: When representing the organization
- Exception: When affecting community members
- Case-by-case evaluation

### Public Figures

Same rules apply to everyone, but:
- Consider broader impact
- May require public statement
- Coordinate with Core Team
- Document thoroughly

### Moderating Moderators

When moderators violate policies:
- Higher standards apply
- Investigation by uninvolved moderators
- Potential removal from moderation team
- Core Team oversight
