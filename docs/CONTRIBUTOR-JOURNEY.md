# Contributor Journey: Your Path in the Philly AI Tech Alliance

This document outlines the progression pathway for community members in the Philly AI Tech Alliance. We believe in recognizing contributions, providing growth opportunities, and building leadership from within our community.

## Journey Overview

```
Newcomer → Contributor → Regular Contributor → Maintainer → Core Team
```

Everyone starts as a newcomer, and through consistent contributions and engagement, you can progress through these stages. This isn't a rigid hierarchy—it's a recognition system that reflects your growing involvement and impact in the community.

---

## Stage 1: Newcomer

**Welcome!** You've just joined the community.

### Characteristics
- New to the community (0-4 weeks)
- Exploring repositories and documentation
- Learning about our projects and goals
- Observing discussions and interactions
- Finding areas of interest

### Typical Activities
- Reading documentation (ONBOARDING.md, CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md, CONTRIBUTING.md)
- Introducing yourself in Discussions or Slack
- Asking questions in Q&A
- Watching repositories of interest
- Attending first community event or meeting

### Engagement Level
- **Time Commitment**: Flexible, self-paced exploration
- **Responsibilities**: None required
- **Recognition**: Welcome posts, introductions acknowledged

### How to Progress
Simply take your first action! Consider:
- Making your first contribution (code, docs, discussion)
- Attending an event or meeting
- Helping answer a question
- Fixing a typo or small issue

### What You Get
- Warm welcome from the community
- Access to all public resources
- Support through mentorship programs
- Clear paths to contribution

---

## Stage 2: Contributor

**You've made your first contribution!**

### Characteristics
- Made 1-5 contributions (1-3 months)
- Familiar with basic workflows (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Learning project-specific conventions
- Building relationships with other contributors
- Finding your niche within the community

### Typical Activities
- Submitting pull requests for code or documentation
- Opening or commenting on issues
- Participating in Discussions
- Attending events or workshops
- Completing good-first-issues
- Asking questions and seeking guidance

### Engagement Level
- **Time Commitment**: 2-6 hours per month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Follow Code of Conduct
  - Respond to feedback on your contributions
  - Communicate if you can't complete assigned work
- **Recognition**:
  - Name in contributor lists
  - Mentioned in release notes (if applicable)
  - Contributor badge on GitHub

### How to Progress to Regular Contributor
Demonstrate consistency and growing expertise:
- Make 5+ meaningful contributions over 2-3 months
- Show understanding of project conventions
- Help other newcomers or contributors
- Participate regularly in Discussions or Slack
- Take initiative on issues beyond good-first-issues

**Timeline**: Typically 2-4 months of consistent contribution

### What You Get
- Recognition as an active contributor
- Increased familiarity with codebases
- Growing network within the community
- Foundation for deeper involvement

---

## Stage 3: Regular Contributor

**You're a familiar face in the community!**

### Characteristics
- Made 10+ contributions (3-6 months)
- Consistent, reliable participation
- Deep knowledge of one or more projects
- Actively helping other contributors
- Trusted by maintainers
- May specialize in specific areas (docs, testing, features, etc.)

### Typical Activities
- Contributing medium-to-complex features or fixes
- Reviewing pull requests from others
- Triaging and labeling issues
- Mentoring newcomers and contributors
- Leading or participating in discussions
- Representing the community at events
- Proposing new features or improvements

### Engagement Level
- **Time Commitment**: 5-10 hours per month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Maintain quality standards in contributions
  - Provide constructive code reviews
  - Help onboard new contributors
  - Communicate availability and commitments
  - Uphold Code of Conduct in interactions
- **Recognition**:
  - Listed in CONTRIBUTORS.md or equivalent
  - May receive special contributor badge/flair
  - Acknowledged in community updates
  - May be featured in blog posts or newsletters

### How to Progress to Maintainer
Show leadership and deep expertise:
- Made 25+ contributions over 4-6 months
- Consistently provide high-quality code reviews
- Demonstrate deep understanding of project(s)
- Show initiative in improving processes or documentation
- Help resolve conflicts or technical debates
- Mentor multiple new contributors successfully
- Participate in project planning or roadmap discussions
- Express interest in maintainer responsibilities

**Timeline**: Typically 6-12 months of regular, high-quality contribution

**Process**:
1. Self-nominate or be nominated by a Core Team member
2. Core Team discusses fit and readiness
3. Decision made via consensus
4. Onboarding period with mentorship from existing maintainer

### What You Get
- Significant influence on project direction
- Deeper relationships with core contributors
- Platform to share expertise
- Preparation for leadership roles

---

## Stage 4: Maintainer

**You're trusted with stewardship of repositories!**

### Characteristics
- 6-12+ months of consistent contribution
- Deep expertise in specific repositories or domains
- Trusted decision-maker for technical and community issues
- Actively shapes project direction
- Mentors and guides other contributors
- May specialize (e.g., Documentation Maintainer, Repository Maintainer)

### Typical Activities
- Reviewing and merging pull requests
- Triaging and prioritizing issues
- Making technical architecture decisions
- Maintaining project roadmaps
- Enforcing coding standards and best practices
- Managing releases and versioning
- Moderating discussions and issues
- Mentoring contributors toward maintainership
- Representing projects in cross-community discussions

### Engagement Level
- **Time Commitment**: 10-20 hours per month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Ensure project health and progress
  - Review PRs within SLA (typically 3-5 business days)
  - Maintain documentation and onboarding materials
  - Uphold Code of Conduct and moderation policies
  - Participate in maintainer meetings
  - Make yourself available for contributor questions
  - Write clear, helpful issue and PR feedback
  - Coordinate with other maintainers
  - Plan and execute releases
- **Recognition**:
  - Listed as maintainer on repositories
  - Write access to repositories
  - Special maintainer badge/flair
  - Featured in community communications
  - May speak at events or conferences on behalf of org

### Permissions
- Write/merge access to assigned repositories
- Ability to manage issues and PRs (label, assign, close)
- Edit access to project boards
- Moderator permissions in Discussions
- May be added to maintainer team on GitHub

### How to Progress to Core Team
Demonstrate organizational leadership:
- 12+ months as effective maintainer
- Significant impact across multiple projects or areas
- Strategic thinking about organization direction
- Active participation in governance discussions
- Mentoring other maintainers
- Building partnerships or initiatives
- Expressing commitment to long-term involvement

**Timeline**: Typically 18-24+ months from first contribution

**Process**:
1. Nomination by existing Core Team member
2. Core Team evaluates contributions and fit
3. Consensus decision required
4. Invitation to join Core Team
5. Onboarding to governance and decision-making processes

### What You Get
- Direct impact on community success
- Authority to make technical decisions
- Platform to mentor and guide others
- Credentials for career advancement
- Deep connections across the community

---

## Stage 5: Core Team

**You're a leader and decision-maker for the organization!**

### Characteristics
- 18+ months of sustained contribution
- Proven leadership in technical and community domains
- Strategic thinker about organization direction
- Trusted by community to make important decisions
- Committed to long-term success of the organization

### Typical Activities
- Governance and strategic decision-making
- Setting organizational direction and priorities
- Managing organizational resources (finances, infrastructure)
- Building partnerships with other organizations
- Representing the organization publicly
- Resolving escalated issues and conflicts
- Mentoring maintainers and senior contributors
- Overseeing major initiatives and workstreams
- Ensuring organizational health and sustainability

### Engagement Level
- **Time Commitment**: 15-30+ hours per month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Participate in governance meetings
  - Vote on major organizational decisions
  - Review and approve budget/resource allocations
  - Handle Code of Conduct escalations
  - Maintain organizational documentation
  - Plan and oversee major initiatives
  - Ensure maintainers have support they need
  - Foster organizational culture
  - Think long-term about sustainability
  - Represent organization ethically and professionally
- **Recognition**:
  - Listed on organization profile as Core Team
  - Owner or admin access to organization
  - Speaker at conferences/events
  - Featured in major announcements
  - Name on organizational materials

### Permissions
- Admin access to GitHub organization
- Full repository permissions across organization
- Access to organizational accounts and infrastructure
- Voting rights on governance decisions
- Financial decision-making authority (if applicable)

### Term and Rotation
- Core Team members serve ongoing terms
- Annual check-in on continued availability and interest
- May step down to Emeritus status if life changes require it
- Emeritus members retain advisory role and recognition

### What You Get
- Significant impact on AI community in Philadelphia
- Leadership experience in open source
- Extensive network across AI and tech communities
- Credentials and recognition as community leader
- Satisfaction of building something meaningful

## Special Roles

In addition to the main progression path, there are specialized roles recognizing specific contributions:

### Documentation Lead
**Focus**: Documentation excellence across the organization

**Responsibilities**:
- Maintain and improve documentation quality
- Review documentation PRs
- Identify documentation gaps
- Coordinate documentation initiatives
- Mentor others on technical writing

**How to Achieve**: Regular contributor with strong documentation contributions; express interest

**Time Commitment**: 5-10 hours/month

---

### Event Organizer
**Focus**: Community events, meetups, workshops

**Responsibilities**:
- Plan and organize community events
- Coordinate speakers and logistics
- Promote events to community
- Facilitate during events
- Gather feedback and iterate

**How to Achieve**: Help with 2-3 events; express interest in leading

**Time Commitment**: 8-15 hours/month (varies by event schedule)

---

### Community Moderator
**Focus**: Maintaining healthy, welcoming community spaces

**Responsibilities**:
- Monitor Discussions, issues, and Slack for Code of Conduct issues
- Respond to reported issues
- Apply moderation policies consistently and fairly
- Escalate serious issues to Core Team
- Help resolve conflicts

**How to Achieve**: Regular contributor; demonstrated maturity and fairness; express interest

**Time Commitment**: 5-8 hours/month

**Requirements**:
- 3+ months active in community
- Trusted by Core Team
- Training on moderation policies

---

### Project Lead
**Focus**: Leading specific projects or initiatives

**Responsibilities**:
- Define project scope and goals
- Coordinate contributors working on the project
- Make technical decisions for the project
- Report progress to maintainers/Core Team
- Ensure project documentation

**How to Achieve**: Propose a new project or take ownership of existing one; approved by Core Team

**Time Commitment**: 10-20 hours/month

## General Progression Principles

### It's Not Linear
- You might progress quickly in some areas and slowly in others
- You can take on special roles at any stage
- Taking breaks is okay—life happens!
- You can move laterally (e.g., Maintainer → Event Organizer)

### It's Not Competitive
- This isn't a race or competition
- Multiple people can be at the same stage
- We succeed when everyone grows
- Helping others progress is valued as highly as personal progression

### It's About Impact, Not Just Volume
- Quality matters more than quantity
- Mentoring others is as valuable as code contributions
- Thoughtful discussion comments count
- Improving processes benefits everyone

### Recognition Is Important
- We celebrate contributions at every level
- Public acknowledgment in releases, newsletters, events
- Private recognition through thank-you messages
- Credentials and recommendations when helpful

### Communication Is Key
- Talk to maintainers about your goals
- Ask for feedback on your progression
- Share your availability and interests
- Let us know if you need support

---

## Stepping Back

### Life Changes
If you need to reduce involvement or step back:
- Let maintainers/Core Team know
- We'll adjust your responsibilities accordingly
- No shame in prioritizing other commitments
- You can return whenever you're ready

### Emeritus Status
For Core Team or long-time Maintainers stepping back:
- Retain recognition and title
- Keep advisory role if desired
- Welcome to return to active status anytime
- Continued access to alumni channels

## Getting Started

**Current Stage**: Probably Newcomer or Contributor

**Next Steps**:
1. Review the stage above where you currently are
2. Identify 2-3 activities to focus on
3. Set a personal timeline (be realistic)
4. Reach out to a maintainer for guidance
5. Start contributing!

**Questions?**
- Open a Discussion in the Q&A category
- Message a maintainer or Core Team member
- Ask in Slack #contributor-journey channel (when available)

---

## Measuring Your Progress

### Self-Assessment Questions
- How many contributions have I made?
- How often do I participate? (weekly, monthly, occasionally)
- Am I helping others in the community?
- Do I understand the project(s) I'm contributing to?
- Am I ready for more responsibility?

### Getting Feedback
- Ask maintainers for feedback on your contributions
- Request a progression discussion (we're happy to talk!)
- Use 1-on-1 office hours with Core Team
- Self-nominate when you feel ready for the next stage

## Examples of Progression

### Example 1: Code Contributor Path
- **Month 1-2**: Newcomer → Fixed 3 typos in docs, completed 2 good-first-issues
- **Month 3-5**: Contributor → Submitted 8 PRs, participated actively in Discussions
- **Month 6-10**: Regular Contributor → Started reviewing PRs, mentored 2 newcomers, implemented 3 features
- **Month 11-15**: Maintainer → Given write access to 2 repos, leading code reviews, planning releases

### Example 2: Community Builder Path
- **Month 1**: Newcomer → Introduced myself, attended meetup
- **Month 2-4**: Contributor → Helped welcome 10+ new members, improved onboarding docs
- **Month 5-8**: Event Organizer (special role) → Organized 2 workshops, grew Slack from 20 to 100 members
- **Month 9-12**: Regular Contributor + Event Organizer → Leading monthly meetups, partnering with local universities
- **Month 13+**: Core Team → Shaping community strategy, representing org at conferences

### Example 3: Hybrid Path
- **Months 1-3**: Newcomer → Contributor through docs and small PRs
- **Months 4-7**: Contributor + Documentation Lead → Rewrote getting-started guides, improved 10 READMEs
- **Months 8-14**: Regular Contributor → Contributing code AND docs, mentoring on technical writing
- **Months 15+**: Maintainer → Maintaining docs repo, reviewing all documentation PRs
